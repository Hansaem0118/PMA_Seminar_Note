\begin{theorem}
There are infinitely many prime numbers.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Let $\mathbb{P}$ be the set of prime numbers. 
Suppose to the contrary that $\mathbb{P}$ is finite.
Then we write
\[
\mathbb{P}=\{p_1, p_2, \ldots, p_n\}.
\]

Set $q=p_1p_2 \cdots p_n+1$. 
Then $q$ is not divisible by any prime number in $\mathbb{P}$.
Thus, every prime factor of $q$ is not in $\mathbb{P}$, a contradiction.

\end{proof}
